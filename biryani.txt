Biryani has Persian roots but is primarily considered an Indian dish, as it was developed and popularized in the Indian subcontinent by the Mughals and through a combination of local influences. The word "biryani" comes from the Persian word birian, meaning "fried before cooking," and the dish is believed to have been introduced to India by Persian and Arab traders who influenced local cuisines. The term is now strongly associated with the Indian subcontinent, where it has evolved into numerous regional variations
